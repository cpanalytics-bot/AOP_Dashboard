# 1. Product Vision

## Problem
Annual planning in field sales is typically done in fragmented spreadsheets. The result
is dirty data, non-comparable plans, no audit trail, no link between plan and actuals,
and a painful consolidation exercise for leadership. Targets are set top-down without a
defensible bottom-up universe and investment rationale.

## Vision statement
> A single, structured, mobile-first system where every territory owner builds a
> bottom-up AOP that is automatically validated, costed, rolled up through the
> reporting hierarchy, approved, and then tracked against actuals in real time.

## Product principles
1. **Bottom-up, hierarchy-aware.** Every BDA plans their territory; BDM and ZDM plans
   are the sum of their lines plus their own initiatives.
2. **Structured, not free-form.** Dropdowns, lookups, and calculated fields wherever
   possible. Free text is the exception, not the rule.
3. **Calculate, don't ask.** Anything derivable (costs, ratios, growth %, ROI) is
   computed by the engine, never typed. This removes arithmetic errors and dirty data.
4. **Plan and actual share one model.** The same dimensions used to plan are used to
   track, so AOP-vs-Actual is a join, not a re-keying exercise.
5. **Mobile-first.** Field staff complete plans on a phone; every screen is designed for
   a single-thumb, vertical flow.
6. **Auditable.** Every status change and approval is logged with who/when/why.

## Target outcomes
- 100% of territories submit a complete, validated AOP before the FY starts.
- Zero arithmetic/dirty-data defects in submitted plans (validation + calc engine).
- Leadership gets a national rollup and zone ranking on day one of the FY.
- Monthly/quarterly reviews run off the same data with no re-collection.

## Scope of FY26-27 v1
In scope: hiring planning, revenue targets, universe, sampling/conversion, training,
investment, review/approval, dashboards, AOP-vs-actual data model.
Out of scope (roadmap): incentive payout engine, AI recommendations, automated
territory optimization (the schema is designed to accommodate these later).
